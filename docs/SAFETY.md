# OpenSkyBook Safety Notes

OpenSkyBook is an advisory planning tool. It is not an official aviation, drone compliance, emergency, navigation, or weather product.

## Core Safety Boundary

OpenSkyBook does not:

- Authorize flight.
- Guarantee airspace status.
- Guarantee obstacle clearance.
- Guarantee aircraft detection.
- Replace official NOTAM sources.
- Replace official weather sources.
- Replace local law, airspace rules, permissions, or pilot judgment.

Use OpenSkyBook as one planning input alongside official sources and direct observation.

## Data May Be Incomplete

Advisory layers can be incomplete, delayed, stale, unavailable, or inaccurate.

This applies to:

- Aircraft traffic.
- ADS-B/network/SDR-derived data.
- Weather and wind estimates.
- NOTAM parsing.
- Power infrastructure.
- Fire detections.
- Map data.
- Heading and location sensors.

## Traffic Awareness

Traffic data can be delayed, filtered, unavailable, or missing aircraft that are not broadcasting or not received by the selected source.

Do not assume an empty map means clear airspace.

## NOTAMs

Parsed NOTAM output is only a review aid. Always verify with official NOTAM and aviation sources before flight.

Manual parsing can miss meaning or context, especially for complex notices.

## Weather And Wind

Weather and wind panels are advisory. Local terrain, gusts, microclimates, and fast-changing conditions can differ from model data.

Use official weather sources and on-site checks for operational decisions.

## Power Infrastructure

Powerline and tower data may be incomplete or stale. Some obstacles may be missing.

Maintain legal and safe separation from all obstacles regardless of whether they appear in the app.

## Fire Detection

NASA FIRMS and similar fire detection sources can be delayed by satellite overpass timing and may miss small, new, obscured, or low-confidence fires.

Use official emergency services and local fire authority sources.

## Alerts

Audio and voice alerts are reminders only. They may not fire, may fire late, or may be based on incomplete data.

Never rely on alerts as the sole hazard-detection method.

## Recommended Operating Practice

Before flight:

1. Check official airspace and NOTAM sources.
2. Check official weather and local conditions.
3. Check landowner, park, event, and local restrictions.
4. Review launch/recovery area and obstacles on site.
5. Confirm RTH height and emergency plan.
6. Treat OpenSkyBook as a planning aid, not final authority.
